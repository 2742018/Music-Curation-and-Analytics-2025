Week 1 – Music Data Theme and Challenges

Task 1: Identifying a Theme for the Dataset

The selected theme for this dataset is Koji Kondo’s compositions for The Legend of Zelda video game series. This theme provides a cohesive focus on one composer’s body of work across multiple decades, game consoles, and stylistic evolutions. It allows analysis of melody, harmony, rhythm, instrumentation, and adaptive scoring techniques within a consistent creative framework.

Task 2: Data Types – Musical Scores, Metadata, and Audio Recordings

This theme enables the gathering of three key data types:

  • Musical Scores: Transcriptions of iconic tracks such as “Overworld Theme” and “Zelda’s Lullaby.”
  • Metadata: Information on release dates, instrumentation, and game contexts sourced from soundtrack archives and fan databases.
  • Audio Recordings: Original game sound files, orchestral re-recordings, and official soundtrack releases available through platforms like YouTube and Spotify.

Task 3: Challenges in Working with Music Data

Working with music-related data presents several challenges, such as accessibility to copyrighted materials, inconsistent metadata standards, and limited availability of clean, analyzable files. In the case of Koji Kondo’s Zelda compositions, these challenges appear in the following ways:

  • Difficulty obtaining official musical scores due to intellectual property restrictions.
  • Variation in metadata between fan transcriptions, soundtrack releases, and game versions.
  • Audio recordings often exist in compressed game formats or mixed versions that complicate frequency and timbre analysis.

Currently, data related to this theme is curated across fan-driven archives, video game music databases, and official Nintendo releases. Each presents the material differently — from sheet music and remastered tracks to metadata tagging systems — showing both the richness and fragmentation of digital music data ecosystems.
