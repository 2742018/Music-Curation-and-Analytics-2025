Task 3 – Comparing histograms and discussion on whether the histograms capture significant differences between the tracks

MFCC Histogram Comparison (max 300 words)

The MFCC histograms provide a quantitative view of how timbre differs across the three tracks. Gerudo Valley, which features prominent guitar and brass, shows relatively broad MFCC distributions, especially in the lower-order coefficients. These wider, multi-peaked shapes indicate a bright spectrum with strong transients and rich harmonic content, consistent with its energetic, rhythmic texture.

In contrast, Kakariko Village has smoother, more tightly concentrated MFCC histograms. Many coefficients cluster around a single dominant region, reflecting its warmer, mid-range instrumentation and more relaxed rhythmic profile. This matches the listening impression of the track as gentle and folk-like rather than percussive.

Midna’s Lament exhibits the most distinct pattern. The lower MFCC coefficients display clear peaks associated with soft piano fundamentals, while higher coefficients remain comparatively small, indicating a darker, less noisy timbre. This aligns with the track’s intimate, melancholic character and sparse arrangement.

Overall, the MFCC histograms capture differences that are clearly audible: bright and aggressive for Gerudo Valley, smooth and centred for Kakariko Village, and subdued for Midna’s Lament. The separations between these histogram shapes suggest that MFCC-based features could be used to discriminate between tracks or to support automatic classification within a larger Zelda soundtrack corpus.
